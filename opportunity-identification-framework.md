# CC/CX Opportunity Identification Framework — One-Pager

**For:** Cisco Sales Engineers, CGEM Inbound (East + West)
**Use:** Spot Contact Center / Customer Experience opportunities inside accounts that don't necessarily *look* like CC accounts.

---

## 1. The reframe — every account is a CX account

Most accounts in CGEM Inbound aren't tagged "Contact Center." That doesn't mean there isn't an opportunity. Almost every customer has at least one of these, and each one is an entry point:

- A **call center** (formal or informal — sales support, claims, dispatch, member services, dealer support, branch back-office).
- A **digital service channel** (web chat, mobile app messaging, WhatsApp, SMS) that someone in the business is responsible for.
- A **frontline workforce** that talks to customers (branches, stores, field techs, advisors, agents).
- A **business outcome under pressure** — retention, NPS, agent attrition, AHT, deflection, regulatory/compliance — that CC + AI can move.

If you find one of those, you have an opportunity worth qualifying.

---

## 2. The 5-question discovery scan

Use these in any meeting — even meetings that aren't about CC. They take ~3 minutes and tell you whether to pull the thread.

1. **"Where in your business do customers talk to a person?"**
   → Surfaces every contact path the customer has, including the ones IT doesn't manage.

2. **"What does the customer experience look like today across those channels — voice, chat, digital, in-person?"**
   → Surfaces fragmentation, channel silos, and inconsistency. Cisco's omnichannel story lands here.

3. **"What are you measuring — and what are you being asked to improve this year?"**
   → Surfaces the business outcome (retention, deflection, AHT, NPS, cost-to-serve). This is what the proposal will be tied to, not features.

4. **"Where are you using — or thinking about using — AI in the customer experience?"**
   → Surfaces AI Agent, AI Agent Assist, self-service automation opportunities. Maps to Webex AI directly.

5. **"What's getting in the way today — vendor, platform, integration, cost, agent experience?"**
   → Surfaces the wedge. Often a CCaaS/UCaaS vendor renewal, a failing IVR, a Salesforce gap, or unsustainable per-agent licensing.

If the customer engages on **three or more** of these, you have a qualified CC/CX opportunity. Bring the right people in.

---

## 3. Find the right person — not the generic IT contact

CC/CX deals do not get bought by the same person who buys collaboration. Map and target deliberately:

| Persona | What they own | Why they care |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **VP / Director of Customer Service / Contact Center Operations** | Agents, queues, SLAs, AHT, retention | Owns the business outcome — the actual buyer |
| **VP / Director of Customer Experience (CX)** | Journey, NPS, channel strategy | Owns the omnichannel and AI story |
| **CIO / CTO / VP IT** | Platform, integration, security, cost | Approves the technical and commercial shape |
| **VP / Director of Digital / Self-Service** | Web, mobile, chatbot, deflection | Owns the AI self-service entry point |
| **Workforce / Operations leader** | Agent experience, scheduling, productivity | Owns AI Agent Assist value |
| **Procurement / Vendor management** | Renewals, contracts | Triggers the buying window when a CCaaS/UCaaS contract is up |

**Rule of thumb:** If your only contact in the account is "IT infrastructure," you do not yet have a qualified CC opportunity. Get a name from the business side — or use one of the personas above as the explicit ask of the AM.

**Tactics for getting in:**
- Ask the AM for an introduction by *role* ("who owns customer service operations?"), not by name.
- Use the Farmers Voice Advantage story as a door-opener — it gives you a credible reason to ask for the right person.
- Use the public hands-on labs (Webex CC + AI, Webex Connect) as a low-friction invitation that the right persona will self-select into.

---

## 4. The opportunity-shape checklist

Before you ask for Cisco resources (CC SE, AI/Connect SE, CX engineering, exec sponsorship), the opportunity should answer **yes** to at least 4 of these:

- [ ] Named business decision-maker engaged (not just IT)
- [ ] Specific business outcome the customer wants to move (retention / deflection / AHT / NPS / cost)
- [ ] Channel scope identified (voice, digital, AI self-service, agent assist, or combination)
- [ ] Trigger / buying window visible (vendor renewal, M&A, regulatory, failing incumbent, AI mandate)
- [ ] Approximate scale known (# agents, # locations, # interactions/month)
- [ ] Customer willing to engage in a workshop, lab session, or POC

4+ yes → bring in the CC/AI specialist resources and start scoping a proposal.
2–3 yes → keep developing; you have a lead, not yet an opportunity.
0–1 yes → it's a conversation, not a deal. Park it and move on.

---

## 5. From opportunity to proposal — the Farmers pattern

When you have a qualified opportunity, shape the proposal the same way the Farmers Voice Advantage program was shaped:

1. **Lead with the business outcome**, not the product (Farmers: "retain at-risk policies" — pilot retained 8 of 8 in week 1).
2. **Make it buyable per unit**, not as a platform megadeal (Farmers per-office model: small / medium / large office monthly cost — the customer can fund one office, then more).
3. **Scope a fundable pilot** with explicit implementation + support cost (Farmers 100-office pilot: $150K implementation + $58K post-implementation support).
4. **Show AI as the differentiator** (AI Agent + AI Agent Assist with privacy-by-design — anonymization and role-based access from day one).
5. **Tie it to expansion** (Farmers pilot → +300 agent licenses → Phase 2 to 2,000 sites → CCaaS finalist).

Reuse this shape. Swap the vertical, swap the metric, swap the scale. The pattern travels.

---

## 6. What to leave the workshop with

Each SE should leave with **one named account**, **one named business decision-maker to target** (or a request to the AM to surface one), and **the next concrete action** (intro request, lab invite, discovery meeting, workshop offer).

That's the unit of progress.
