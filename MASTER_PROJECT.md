# CC/CX Opportunity Workshop

**Owner:** Ed Villarreal
**Audience:** Cisco Sales Engineers across CGEM Inbound (East + West)
**Format:** ≤1-hour module within a broader half-day workshop
**Status:** Scaffolding

---

## Goal

Equip CGEM Inbound SEs (East and West) to:

1. **Identify** Contact Center / Customer Experience opportunities inside their existing accounts.
2. **Qualify** those opportunities by reaching the right business and IT decision-makers — not just general IT contacts.
3. **Construct** a credible customer-facing proposal (problem → Cisco solution → commercial shape → next steps).

The East region historically has had less CC activity than the West; this workshop is part of the motion to change that.

---

## Tie to broader work

- Supports **Prompt 2 / Goal 1** of Ed's P&D reflection: "Build out the contact center business in CGEM Inbound East."
- Reuses proven assets: Farmers Voice Advantage story, per-office commercial model, public hands-on labs (Webex CC + AI, Webex Connect Flow Builder), Webex CC exec deck and demo.

---

## Module shape (1 hour)

| Time | Section | Output |
|------|---------|--------|
| 0–10 min | Why CC/CX is a Cisco opportunity right now (AI, Webex AI, vendor disruption in CCaaS) | Shared frame |
| 10–25 min | Opportunity-identification framework — how to spot CC/CX inside an account that doesn't look like a CC account | One-pager (this project) |
| 25–40 min | Reaching the right decision-makers — CC operations, CX leaders, CIO/CTO orgs (vs. generic IT) | Talk-track + role map |
| 40–55 min | From conversation to proposal — Farmers playbook applied to a generic account | Proposal-shape walkthrough |
| 55–60 min | Wrap + commitments — each SE leaves with one named account to apply this to | Commitment list |
